T1 — Book Voice: all serif (EB Garamond)

The full 1930s homage. Gorgeous at display sizes, unmistakably "book." Weaknesses: digits and hex codes are less crisp at small sizes, and it diverges from your personal brand entirely.

T2 — Brand Voice: all Atkinson Hyperlegible

Fully consistent with your personal site, maximum legibility, and hex/RGB codes get the matching Hyperlegible Mono — genuinely nicer for a copy-the-code tool. Weakness: loses the bookish, era feel; the japandi mood must come entirely from color and spacing.

T3 — Hybrid: serif for names, Hyperlegible for everything else (Recommended)

EB Garamond speaks only where the book speaks — the wordmark and the color names, at sizes where it's beautiful. Atkinson Hyperlegible handles UI, labels, and body; Hyperlegible Mono handles the codes. The book keeps its voice, your brand keeps its hands on the controls, and each font works only where it's strongest.
